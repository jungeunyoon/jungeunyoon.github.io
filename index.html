WEATHERVAIN

Winner of "Best Overall App" and "Most Original App" at the 2014 UT Mobile Apps Demo Day!

"Make your morning routine easier with WeatherVain! WeatherVain will help you dress for the crazy weather by bringing you your current weather forecast each day with an outfit suggestion. It’s the best way to be prepared for whatever the circumstance is outside."


ABOUT WEATHERVAIN:

Project Type: iOS Mobile Application

Dates: January 2014 - April 2014

Team: Six members consisting of three CS an three Journalism students.

Tools Used: Xcode 5, Objective-C, TestFlight, CocoaPods, Parse, OpenWeatherMap API, Trello, Github, Git, Photoshop CS5, Illustrator, Final Cut Pro

Role: On the computer science students' end, we had a little less than two months to pick up iOS development without any instruction. My job was to tie everything together, in both a programming aspect and a UI/Front-End aspect, with the use of Parse and Cocoapods. I also developed the outfit matching algorithm that maneuvers the clothing databases, taking into consideration the "feels like" temperature with respect to wind speed, the average temperature within a scope of X hours, the user rating system of individual items of clothing, and the cohesiveness of the clothes themselves. here ended up being over 5000 outfit possibilities. We collectively also taught non-programmer teammates how to code.

Value: 1. Communication & Problem Solving - This fast-paced class makes you fully realize that people naturally think differently. It forces you to learn how to be a better communicator and pushes you be a team player. It gave us a sense of the real world work environment.

2. Quick Learning - It taught us to learn and adapt quickly. We had a little less than two months to pick up iOS mobile development. It was either pick it up very quickly by investing time outside of classes or not have an app ready by demo day.

WEATHERIT

"Inspired by WeatherVain but created completely from scratch in order to learn Swift and to be able to implement the other parts of the app that I had not been exposed to previously."

ABOUT WEATHERIT:

Project Type: iOS Mobile Application

Dates: Late September 2015 - current

Team: Solo

Tools Used: Xcode 6, Swift, some minor Objective-C, TestFlight, CocoaPods, Parse, OpenWeatherMap API, Trello, Github, Git, Photoshop CS5, Illustrator

Status: I'm currently finishing up the UI/UX and making improvements to the algorithm as of September 28th and plan to submit to the app store within the end of the month.

Value:
1. Willingness to Learn - I could have easily reused code and graphics from WeatherVain but I started from scratch because I really wanted to get a chance to implement the other parts of the app that I hadn't been able to previously with WeatherVain. More specifically, I wanted to set up the entire closet.

2. Swift - I started this project to learn Swift. Though I had dabbled with the new language before, I hadn't had a chance to actually use it on a project.

3. Dedication - The WeatherVain team went our separate ways so we never had a chance to fix what was necessary to make it to the app store. However, one thing about me is that once I start something I always have to follow through. It was always my intention to release a version of the original app so I made time. Working on WeatherIt after graduation shows that I am dedicated and passionate enough to finish projects.

4. Design - I completely reworked the UI/UX for WeatherIt so that I could experiment with new styles. For instance, the app features a slide-out navigation menu rather than WeatherVain's use of a UITabBarViewController.

HATFANCY

"A series of educational projects for CS373: Software Engineering at the University of Texas at Austin with Professor Glenn Downing in summer 2015, organized into three phases. Our goal was to 'create a Web app hosted on Rackspace that emulates IMDB to track something'. Dota 2 players commonly trade and sell items that they have acquired. This project's goal is to provide a gateway to a database that lists the price data of these items, among other things."

ABOUT HATFANCY:

Project Type: Web Application

Dates: June 2015 - July 2015

Team: 5 members, "ItTakesFiveToDjango"

Tools Used: Rackspace, Apiary, Python, Javascript, Django, PostgreSQL, CSS, HTML, Bootstrap, Django, jQuery, Github, Git, Slack

Role: Led the team for Phase I (of three) and was the overall task organizer for subsequent weeks. I quickly learned Twitter Bootstrap and created a custom reactive front end within two days. I also wrote 85% of the 6000+ word technical report and created the data models and the UML diagrams.

Value:
1. Collaboration - My team members and I were constantly communicating through Slack during those three weeks. One person would point out a minor bug and we, as a group, would get them fixed right away. It showed me even further how beneficial it is to work in a collaborative and supportive setting.

2. Adaptivity - We started off using a Bootstrap template because our professor encouraged all the groups to do so. He didn't see the value in any of us creating custom layouts and frankly, he didn't think that it was possible for us to come up with them in such a short amount of time and in the end, no other team used anything other than themes found online. However, I wanted to use the time that was given to actually learn so I spent three days learning Twitter Bootstrap (D-1), designing the website and making the graphics (D-2), and implementing the design (D-3). It was a great learning experience on how adaptive you have to be in the real world since job requirements and software is constantly changing.

FORUMS

"I taught myself how to code (HTML/CSS/Javascript) when I was 11 through managing forums. Though most of the forums are now gone, I was able to retrieve a few 'skins' that I had made."

ABOUT FORUMS:

Project Type:

Dates: October 2004 - December 2013

Team: Solo

Tools Used: HTML, CSS, Javascript, DoHTML, Jcink, Invisionfree, Proboards, Photoshop

Role: Every single aspect of the forum has to be created. It takes quite a bit of HTML/CSS and javascript to create a custom 'skin' for forums because Jcink [forum hosting] is very pliable to customization. It's not just the header and sidebar that needs to be designed and coded. You also have to think about what the admin post template will look like, what the member's mini profile will look like, and make the user experience intuitive.

Value:
1. Market Awareness - It made me aware of the ever-changing market and how important it is to keep up with new design trends. One month fixed sidebars would be the standard and the next month modal sidebars would push forward as the favorite. This always keep me on my toes and made me learn something new every few weeks. I even started a few trends myself, including the use of jQuery tabs inside a sticky sidebar.
